## Overview

This repository contains a professional Phishing Detection & Awareness Report completed as part of the Cyber Security Internship at Future Interns.

The report analyzes 4 real-world style phishing email samples, identifies common phishing indicators, classifies risk levels, and provides practical awareness guidelines to help individuals and organizations protect themselves from phishing attacks.

## Objective

- Analyze phishing email samples for common attack indicators
- Classify each email by risk level (Safe / Suspicious / Phishing)
- Explain how each attack works in simple, non-technical language
- Provide clear prevention tips and employee awareness guidelines

## Emails Analyzed

| # | Email Type | Attack Category | Risk Level |
|---|-----------|----------------|------------|
| 1 | Fake PayPal Security Alert | Brand Impersonation | Critical |
| 2 | Fake IT Password Reset | Credential Harvesting | Critical |
| 3 | Fake Bank Loan Approval | Advance Fee Fraud | Critical |
| 4 | Fake CEO Wire Transfer | Business Email Compromise (BEC) | Critical |

## Key Findings

- 4/4 emails confirmed as phishing attempts
- 25+ red flags identified across all samples
- Attack types range from simple brand impersonation to sophisticated BEC attacks
- Most dangerous finding: BEC attack containing no malicious links - relies purely on social engineering

## Phishing Indicators Covered

1. Fake / Spoofed Sender Domain
2. Urgency and Fear Language
3. Generic Greeting
4. Suspicious / Malicious Links
5. Credential Harvesting
6. Secrecy Demand
7. Too Good To Be True Offers
8. Blocks Verification Attempts
9. Advance Fee Fraud
10. Business Email Compromise (BEC)

## Disclaimer

All phishing email samples in this report are simulated examples created strictly for educational purposes as part of a Cyber Security Internship. No real phishing emails were sent, no systems were attacked, and no individuals were harmed. All analysis was conducted passively and ethically in compliance with the internship scope and guidelines.
